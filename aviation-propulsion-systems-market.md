<p><p>The aviation propulsion systems market comprises two main types: air-breathing and non-air-breathing systems. Air-breathing systems, such as turbojets and turbofan engines, utilize ambient air for combustion, making them suitable for conventional aircraft. In contrast, non-air-breathing systems, like rocket engines, carry both fuel and oxidizer, enabling operation outside the Earth's atmosphere. These systems are essential for space exploration and high-speed flight. Each type caters to specific operational needs, driving advancements in efficiency and performance within the aviation sector.</p></p>
