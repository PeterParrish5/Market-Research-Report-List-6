<p><p>Hemoglobin A1c testing devices for diabetes utilize various technologies to measure glycated hemoglobin levels, reflecting long-term blood sugar control. Boronate affinity chromatography selectively captures A1c variants, while direct enzymatic assays quantify hemoglobin directly using enzymatic reactions. Immunoassays employ antibodies for specific A1c detection, whereas ion-exchange HPLC separates hemoglobin types based on charge differences. Micro-optical detection methods utilize light to analyze samples, and capillary electrophoresis separates molecules based on their size and charge, offering a range of precision and efficiency in monitoring diabetes management.</p></p>
