<p><p>The high-voltage power cable market encompasses three main types: underground, overhead, and submarine cables. Underground cables are installed below ground to minimize environmental impact and increase safety, commonly used in urban areas. Overhead cables are suspended from poles or towers, facilitating easy access for maintenance and repairs, often used in rural and suburban settings. Submarine cables are laid on the seabed for long-distance energy transmission between continents or islands, crucial for connecting offshore power sources to the grid.</p></p>
