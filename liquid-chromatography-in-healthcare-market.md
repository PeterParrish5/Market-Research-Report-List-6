<p><p>Liquid chromatography is a technique used in healthcare to separate and analyze components of a mixture. There are several types of liquid chromatography used in the healthcare market including ion-exchange chromatography, partition chromatography, size-exclusion chromatography, and supercritical fluid chromatography. Ion-exchange chromatography separates ions based on their charge, while partition chromatography separates compounds based on their affinity for a stationary phase. Size-exclusion chromatography separates molecules based on their size, and supercritical fluid chromatography uses supercritical fluids as the mobile phase for separation.</p></p>
