<p><p>A Distributed Temperature Sensing System is a technology that measures temperature along a fiber optic cable by detecting changes in light intensity. There are two main types of fibers used in this system: Single-Mode Fiber and Multimode Fiber. </p><p>Single-Mode Fiber is designed for long-distance communication and allows light to travel in one mode, resulting in high precision temperature measurements. Multimode Fiber, on the other hand, is suitable for shorter distances and allows multiple modes of light to travel, providing less accurate temperature readings but lower costs.</p></p>
