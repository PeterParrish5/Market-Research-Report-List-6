<p><p>Implantable drug infusion pumps are devices designed to deliver medications directly into a patient's body, ensuring precise dosing and control. Patient-Controlled Analgesia (PCA) pumps allow patients to manage pain relief by self-administering doses. Enteral pumps deliver nutrition or medications directly into the gastrointestinal tract. Insulin pumps provide continuous insulin delivery for diabetes management. Elastomeric pumps are portable devices that use elasticity to deliver medication over time. Syringe pumps precisely control the infusion of medication from a syringe, often used in clinical settings.</p></p>
