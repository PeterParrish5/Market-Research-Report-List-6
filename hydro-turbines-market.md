<p><p>Hydro turbines are devices used to convert the kinetic energy of flowing water into mechanical energy, which is then used to generate electricity. There are several types of hydro turbines used in the market, including Francis, Kaplan, Pelton, and others. Each type has its own specific design and operating characteristics, making them suitable for different hydroelectric power generation projects. Francis turbines are commonly used for medium to high-head applications, while Kaplan turbines are ideal for low to medium-head sites. Pelton turbines are specifically designed for high-head installations, utilizing the impulse principle. Other types of hydro turbines include Archimedes screw turbines, Banki-Michell turbines, and Turgo turbines.</p></p>
