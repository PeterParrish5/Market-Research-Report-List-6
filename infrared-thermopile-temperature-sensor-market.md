<p><p>Infrared thermopile temperature sensors are devices that measure temperature by detecting infrared radiation emitted by objects. These sensors consist of multiple thermocouples connected in series, allowing them to produce a voltage signal proportional to the temperature of the object. <p><p>The infrared thermopile temperature sensor market is divided into two main types: through hole thermopile infrared sensors and SMD (Surface-Mount Device) thermopile infrared sensors. Through hole sensors are designed for insertion into circuit boards, offering robust connections and stability in various applications. In contrast, SMD sensors are compact and facilitate automated assembly, making them ideal for high-density applications. Each type serves distinct needs, with through hole sensors often used in industrial settings and SMD sensors favored for consumer electronics and portable devices.</p></p>
